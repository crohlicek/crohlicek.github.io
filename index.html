What's my background?

I completed my Bachelor's degree at Harvard, where I studied Applied Math with a focus in signal processing. In my first couple of years I spent most of my time working on classes in the pure math department, before shifting to a more applied end of the mathematical spectrum in the latter years of my program. Ending my undergraduate career with classes and internships in topics like decision theory, speech/image recognition, and mathematical machine learning, I'm most interested in applications of applied mathematics to modeling physical and biological systems, and exploring ways to make neural networks more efficient.

What am I working on?

I'm an Associate Computational Biologist at the Broad Institute working in Dr. Victoria Popic's lab on applications of deep learning to structural variant calling in the genome. Our work in the lab frames an important problem in biology in a new light that makes use of ideas from signal processing and machine learning and makes an important step towards expanding the use of data-driven algorithms in the field rather than hand-crafted heuristics.
