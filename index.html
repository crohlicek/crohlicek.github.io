What's my background?

I completed my Bachelor's degree at Harvard, where I studied Applied Math with a focus in signal processing. In my first couple of years I spent most of my time working on classes in the pure math department, before shifting to a more applied end of the mathematical spectrum in the latter years of my program. Ending my undergraduate career with classes and internships in topics like decision theory, speech/image recognition, and mathematical machine learning, I'm most interested in ways of applying abstract mathematical principles to make neural networks more efficient.

What am I working on?

I'm currently getting my Master's in Data Science at Brown University, completing coursework in statistical machine learning and data engineering. In addition to my classes, I'm also a research assistant at the Carney Institute for Brain Science at Brown, where I'm researching methods for dynamical systems reduction with recurrent neural networks. I'm fascinated by different methods of model complexity reduction, and the dynamical domain of experimental neurophysiological data is rich with mathematical insights.
